Collaboration model guides, SPV guides, FAQ, and how-to articles

What is Project-Based Collaboration?

Best for delivering a specific project with one or more partners. Includes task-based engagements, consortiums, project JVs, and Special Purpose Vehicles (SPV). Ideal when scope and timeline are well defined.

What are Strategic Partnerships?

Long-term alliances: strategic joint ventures, strategic alliances, and mentorship programs. Suited when you want ongoing collaboration rather than a single project.

What is Resource Pooling?

Reduce costs by sharing resources: bulk purchasing, equipment sharing, and resource exchange. Useful for companies with complementary needs.

What are Hiring & Competitions?

Engage professionals or consultants, or participate in RFPs and competitions to win work. Use when you need talent or want to compete for projects.

What is an SPV?

Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs) are used for project-specific legal entities. They allow partners to isolate risk and structure financing for a single project or asset.

Do I need legal advice for an SPV?

Yes. Consult your legal and tax advisors when setting up an SPV. PMTwin does not provide legal advice.
